Fried Chicken

    Ingredients:
  • 4 pieces boneless chicken breast - skin on
  • 1 quart seasoned flour (see tips for more information)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • hot sauce
  • 2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce

If using a deep fryer, fill fryer oil to the recommended line and turn temperature to 350 degrees. If using a cast-iron or other skillet, turn heat between medium and medium-high. Allow skillet to get hot then add enough oil to cover half way up the chicken. Use a submersive thermometer to measure the temperature of the oil and adjust heat source accordinly. You don't want your oil getting above 325 in the cast iron. While the oil is heating, let your chicken come up to room temperature on the counter. Next combine the eggs, worcestershire, hot sauce, and a splash of water in a medium size bowl and set aside. Split the seasoned flour into two separate medium sized bowl and set aside - one on each side of the egg wash. When your oil reaches the desired temperature, dredge your chicken in flour, then egg-wash, then back in flour as described in the breading station section on the tips page. After your chicken is breaded, gently place your breaded chicken into the skillet (see tips for how to do so without splattering grease back on yourself) or put in fryer basket and submerge in deep fryer. Cook until the internal temperature, measured with a probing thermometer, is 160 degrees. Let your chicken rest for 5-10 minutes (carry over cooking will finish cooking the chicken to 165 degrees without drying it out while also allowing the muscles and juices to relax, which will prevent the juices from running out when you cut or bite into it)
